One of my top 2 or 3 books. Just loved it.

The first time I read it, I was struck by this picture of humbly ministering to a community in spite of feeling deeply hurt by a member, even a person in the community you love. Just wrecked me.

Second reading, I'm really struck by how it explores the distance between 2 generations (parent and child), the difference between being known and understood (we tend to know our parents exceptionally well, but often don't understand them), and this desire for our kids to understand us.
